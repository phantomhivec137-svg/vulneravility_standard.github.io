Definition

CVSS is an industry standard designed to assess and communicate the severity of software vulnerabilities in a standardized way. It was initially developed under the direction of the National Infrastructure Advisory Council (NIAC) in 2003/2004 and was first released in February 2005.

Main Objective

To provide an open and universally standard way to rate the severity of security vulnerabilities.

Standard Assessment

Allows end users to understand the severity of a vulnerability through a numerical score from 0 to 10, facilitating objective comparison.

Severity, not Risk

Designed to measure technical severity (impact on confidentiality, integrity, availability) rather than actual exploitation risk.

Prioritization

Helps organizations prioritize the response and remediation of critical vulnerabilities based on the resulting score.

Transparency & Consistency

Maintains a common language among vendors, researchers, and consumers, eliminating threat communication ambiguity.

Key Application Areas

Risk Prioritization

Helps organizations decide which vulnerabilities to fix first, integrating technical severity with asset criticality.

Industry Standard

Universally used by databases like CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) and NVD (National Vulnerability Database).

Diverse Environments

CVSS v4.0 explicitly covers IT, Operational Technology (OT), Industrial Control Systems (ICS), and IoT devices.

Compliance and Reporting

Facilitates audit reports and regulatory compliance by providing measurable metrics to compare risks.

Advantages

Developed and managed by FIRST, offering key benefits for cybersecurity.

Standardization and Comparability

Provides a common language and objective metrics to compare vulnerability severity across products, eliminating subjective manufacturer methods.

Efficient Prioritization

With scores from 0 to 10, it facilitates rapid identification of critical weaknesses, focusing resources on immediate risks.

Comprehensive Context

Uses Base, Temporal, and Environmental metrics to evaluate intrinsic severity, patch availability, and specific environment impact.

Universal Adoption

An open, free, and widely used standard integrated with vulnerability management tools and public databases like NVD.

Real Example

Shellshock (CVE-2014-6271)

Obtained a base score of 10.0 in CVSS v3.0 due to its nature of remote code execution without privileges or user interaction.
